Skills and Self Education

While I must still eat, so I cannot devote 100% of my time to self learning, a considerable portion of my time when there are no jobs available or I am simply not at work, is devoted to improving and enriching my skills. This is not always reflected on GitHub as unlike many users of that site, I do not push things that do not work onto the internet for people to see and hopefully improve. Instead I make them functional first and foremost, as I am a solo developer who is not at present on a development team but producing all of these work products and the 200+ pieces of digital art I have made in the last 3 months _by myself_. This should demonstrate an ability to produce massive amounts of content quickly and without reliance on others while the 6 years I worked in management for a retail store should demonstrate my team work capacity.

Improving my skills is not something that I do begrudgingly as I find that to develop myself is instead an enriching and rewarding process and why I am even now devoting the amounts of time that I do to development, often at the expense of finding additional jobs or selling more components on eBay, as of the moment I do the amount of work necessary to eat, retain some stable residence and provide for my transportation and upon reaching my "magic number" devote the rest of my time to improving these skills.

This focus on self-improvement is a salient portion of my character that is not likely to change with more lucrative or demanding employment but is instead enduring in that I have found portions of time in which I do not do this to be unrewarding and boring in their duration.

Long Term Goals
  • Masters in CSUntil more lucrative employment is available to me, I cannot justify returning for a more advanced degree but after a few years of working and stabilizing my financial bedrock, I plan to pursue a Masters or potentially PhD in computer science and pursue more low-level OS design paths than my current knowledge enables either in more functional embedded or consumer desktop operating systems as macOS and Windows still leave much to be desired while Linux lacks the necessary polish to become more mainstream (while developers may not mind building from the source, this is often not explained well enough for the average business user who simply needs the computer to function and at present grapples with Windows to achieve her aims).
  • Electric Tantra Design ParadigmA current focus that this site is a monumental step towards is a desktop/mobile design paradigm for consumer facing apps and websites I have given the moniker the Electric Tantra. The idea is to create a cyberpunk/retrowave design paradigm (such as Google's Material Design paradigm) that is easily deployed, less garish than Google's vibrant alternative, slick and functional without needing much more than an explanation and sets of color palettes to achieve. The paradigm rests in the use of either router or modal functionality to hide away the chunks of content behind pleasant looking and easily found buttons, as you have seen on this site, which are as easy to use and natural if clicked by a finger or mouse. Eventually this may become a Javascript library I distribute in minified form or simply a set of color pallets and explanation I offer on some site and develop exclusively with when developing for myself.
  • Electric Tantra OSFinding myself unsatisfied with all Linux distros in the course of using them, especially the popular but not highly functional Arch family, I have begun working on a Ubuntu based OS of my own to simplify the reinstallation process of the particular environments I use. Like many other such projects, it is called the Electric Tantra as well and part of my creation thereof has been focused on applying the design principles I use for sites on the OS itself. While still in an early alpha phase, I am using Ubuntu as the base operating system, as it has the PPA functionality that makes it so popular with users for compatibility purposes, the APT package manager which is robust and well tested and the APT-FAST extension to make APT perform more like the Alpine Package Manager. On top of that OS base, I am using the DE provided by the Solus Project, Budgie, for it provides the extensibility and function of the Gnome 2 environment with compatibility with Gnome extensions without the awful overhead that the Gnome 3 environment is saddled with. While I use workstations which can take as much punishment as the GNU organization can dish out, I like using older laptops (no fear of having them stolen as they are easily replaced unlike MacBooks) so I do not want a DE that is too heavy nor broken as the Plasma base usually implies.

Design: Introduction and Considerations

My gallery can be found here

Digital Art Style:Art is a recent venture born of necessity in developing the Resurgence site that has picked up considerable steam since, as it happens I am much better at creating aesthetically pleasing pieces when I am not using my trembling hands to do so but instead a mouse and keyboard. This has lead me to explore the various approaches I have to my favorite subjects of my unusual style of photography. I attempt to show things to people as I see those them while retaining the perspective of human's being a small part of a bigger world by capturing as much of the sky and the image will allow for. The use of editing is not to enhance the picture but instead impart upon the viewer the feelings I felt when tasking it, which is easy to remember for me because the picture reminds me immediately of what I was feeling when I took it.

Site UI/Ux: This particular combination of personalized artwork and deliberatively simple to make it so hiring managers, or prospective clients, as easy an experience as possible considering the multitude of candidates they go over per day.The dark color pattern is to make the site easier to maintain in the night, as it does not cause strain on the eyes. Furthermore, the neon-black contrast employed was chosen deliberately to pull in visual cues from "cyberpunk" and especially "retrowave" both of which are stylistic paradigms associated with distinct types of electronic music (which I otherwise am not a fan of, but the aesthetics are beautiful and I am able to adapt them into my designs and art)

The key to this pages design is to introduce some topics and cover some extraneous bits of pertinent information(like employment history and education) without creating a cluttered mess of paragraphs. This improves the lives of the customer but not ruining one's eyes from excessive brightness if seen at night (often when I have time to work on the project after working). This was achieved using "Modal Windows" which come with HTML5.
